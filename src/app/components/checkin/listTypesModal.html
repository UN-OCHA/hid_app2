What are these?

You can check into different types of contact lists. To easier find the ones you want to check in, choose a type of list first. These are the different types:

Operations

Operations usually refer to single countries, like Afghanistan or Iraq. In some cases they also refer to a bigger humanitarian response across borders, for example the Whole of Syria.

Groups

Groups can be clusters, sectors or simply working groups that are set up in a specific country to facilitate coordination among humanitarian partners.

Disasters

Disasters usually refer to sudden-onset emergencies in a country or region. The Nepal Earthquake, the Haiti Hurricane Matthew or the floods in the Balkans are just a few examples.

Organizations

You should only check into an organization that you belong to. All organizations are moderated, i.e. your check-in will have to be approved by a Humanitarian ID manager.

Roles

Roles are functional positions used in a humanitarian response. Very common roles are the Humanitarian Coordinator, Information Management Officers or UNDAC members for example. As for organizations, these check-ins are moderated.

Co-ordination hub

A co-ordination hub is an area that does not correspond to official administrative boundaries. Instead it is hub (usually a bigger city) where coordination takes place. Often times, an OSOCC is set up in a sudden-onset disaster and becomes the co-ordination hub a few weeks after the disaster strikes.
