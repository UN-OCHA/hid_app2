What are these?

You can check into different types of contact list. First, choose which list or lists are most appropriate for you. Here are the different types:

Operation

Operations usually refer to single countries, like Afghanistan or Iraq. In some cases they also refer to a wider humanitarian response across borders, for example the Whole of Syria response.

Group

Groups can be clusters, sectors or simply working groups that are set up in a specific country to facilitate coordination among humanitarian partners.

Disaster

Disasters usually refer to sudden-onset emergencies in a country or region. The Nepal Earthquake, Hurricane Matthew in Haiti or the floods in the Balkans are just a few examples.

Organization

You should only check into an organization that you belong to. All organizational lists are moderated, i.e. your check-in will have to be approved by a Humanitarian ID manager.

Role

Roles are closely related to your job title. Very common roles are those of Humanitarian Coordinator, Information Management Officer or UNDAC member. As with checking into organizational lists, role check-ins are moderated.

Coordination hub

A coordination hub is a location where coordination takes place, usually in or outside a large city. For instance, more often than not an OSOCC set up in a sudden-onset disaster becomes a coordination hub for a few weeks after a disaster strikes.
